START ECON DEPT

- Department values: rigorous, math- and quantitative-driven economic analysis applied to policy areas (poverty, environment/energy, globalization/trade, education, innovation, and economic history); strong emphasis on publishing, external grants, student training, and inclusive excellence.
- Core research topics: poverty and inequality, migration and labor, energy and environmental markets (fuel/carbon), international trade and tariffs, economic history, econometrics, and economic theory.
- High-value co-author and collaboration opportunities:
    - Center for Poverty and Inequality Research — co-directed by Marianne Page (Econ) and Jacob Hibel (Sociology): interdisciplinary poverty-policy research, graduate/undergraduate involvement, strong opportunity for co-authored empirical work and grant proposals.
    - Global Migration Cluster — led by Giovanni Peri: multi-discipline migration projects (economics, sociology, political science, history, demography, law); good source of co-authors for migration, labor, and policy work.
    - Davis Energy Economics Program (DEEP) — James Bushnell, David Rapson, Erich Muehlegger: energy/environment markets, carbon/fuel markets, consumer behavior and IO; frequent policy- and industry-collaboration opportunities and co-authorships.
    - All‑UC Group in Economic History — active UC-wide faculty and grads; contact Christopher Meissner for UC collaborations and historical-trade research partnerships (historical ties to Greg Clark).
    - Center for International Data — directed by Robert Feenstra: rich trade/import-export/ tariff data resources and student-supported empirical projects useful for co-authored trade research.
- Teaching and mentoring groups: active undergraduate and graduate programs, integration with joint business/managerial majors, and student involvement through centers/clusters—strong opportunities to co-supervise MS/PhD students and integrate research into courses.
- Institutional supports: regular publication/grant activity among faculty, cross-disciplinary workshops/conferences, and outreach via Cooperative Extension—facilitates multi-author projects, student research assistants, and policy engagement.


END ECON DEPT
