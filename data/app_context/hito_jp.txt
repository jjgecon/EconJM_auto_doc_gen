START ECON DEPT
- The Institute of Economic Research (IER) at Hitotsubashi University is a leading center for comprehensive research on the Japanese and global economy, with about thirty faculty members and a strong commitment to rigorous, open, and collaborative research.
- IER hosts a vibrant international research community, welcoming numerous visiting scholars and fostering joint research with both domestic and overseas researchers, offering ample opportunities for co-authorship and collaboration.
- Major research groups and activities include:
    - Productivity measurement and international comparison (in collaboration with METI and overseas partners)
    - Income inequality and long-term economic data analysis (including international comparisons)
    - Survey-based studies on economic behavior, health, and labor markets
    - Empirical macroeconomic and financial analysis using advanced statistical methods
    - Policy-oriented research on asset bubbles, inflation expectations, and economic uncertainty
    - Micro-level development economics research in Asian countries
    - Analysis of government policy impacts, globalization, and labor/environmental issues
    - Specialized research on the Russian and former Soviet economies
    - Construction and provision of large-scale economic and historical data sets
- The IER is recognized as a national research hub, supporting collaborative projects and providing access to extensive economic data resources for researchers and students.


END ECON DEPT
