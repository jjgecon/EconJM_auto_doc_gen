START JOB DESCRIPTION

- Two tenure-track faculty positions (Assistant/Associate Professor) in Agricultural, Food, and Applied Economics at Purdue University in Indianapolis.
- Candidates should have a Ph.D. in agricultural economics, business management, managerial economics, applied microeconomics, marketing, or a related discipline by the start date.
- Evidence of excellence in scholarship, with potential for national distinction (especially for Associate Professor with tenure).
- Expected to develop a nationally recognized research and teaching program in areas such as agricultural economics, agribusiness management, food business management, or agricultural/environmental/food policy.
- Strong record or potential for publishing in high-impact peer-reviewed journals and securing external grant funding.
- Ability to mentor graduate students.
- Teaching responsibilities include undergraduate and graduate courses (average two per year), with potential for distance-delivered courses.
- Engagement with agricultural and food industry leaders, policymakers, and stakeholders is highly valued.
- Opportunities to collaborate with departmental centers (e.g., Center for Food and Agricultural Business, Center for Commercial Agriculture).

END JOB DESCRIPTION

- The Innovation Hub aims to make Purdue the most innovative residential program among large research universities.
- Focuses on advancing teaching and learning, adding value to student experiences, and fostering a passion for learning.

START ECON DEPT

- Department values interdisciplinary research, excellence in teaching, and engagement with industry and policy stakeholders.
- Research topics include agribusiness management, consumer behavior, sales, experimental economics, industrial organization, decision sciences, development, trade, policy, macroeconomics, production, consumption, and environmental/resource issues.
- Large and diverse faculty (34 tenure track) and student body (over 420 undergraduates, 100+ graduates) provide opportunities for collaboration and co-authorship.
- The department is part of the College of Agriculture, which is globally ranked and committed to teaching, research, and extension missions.
- Potential research and teaching groups include departmental centers such as the Center for Food and Agricultural Business and the Center for Commercial Agriculture.

END ECON DEPT
