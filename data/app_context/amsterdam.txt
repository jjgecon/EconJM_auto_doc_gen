START ECON DEPT

- The Amsterdam School of Economics (ASE) is a leading European research institute with a strong emphasis on outstanding research and world-class teaching.
- ASE fosters an ambitious and supportive culture, offering opportunities for personal development in an international environment.
- Researchers are highly active in international academic networks and collaborate within the Tinbergen Institute, ASE’s graduate school.
- The Quantitative Economics section leads most quantitative economics courses and produces internationally recognized research.
- Research programs include Actuarial Science & Mathematical Finance, Econometrics, and Equilibrium, Expectations & Dynamics (CeNDEF).
- The Experimental & Political Economics group focuses on behavioral determinants of economic decision-making, political economy, and laboratory experiments.
- Potential co-authors and collaborators span across these research groups, with particular strengths in political participation, voting, and interest group influence.
